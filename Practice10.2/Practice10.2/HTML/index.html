Hobbies

What I live

Art

Art for me is communication with people of different years, nationalities, eras. Pictures are a way to feel a person’s soul, to appreciate his character. And reading books is a journey that does not require large expenditures and efforts. Any expression of emotions created by man is inspiration and my own source of vitality.

The Universe

From early childhood Astronomy is one of the most important hobbies in my life. It’s hard and exciting to imagine that somewhere in the dark wanderers of huge size “float”, each with their own story. But this science allows you to look through time and space, discover new worlds and realize that the boundaries of the possible do not exist.

The Earth

I believe that our planet is fraught with no less miracles than the entire universe as a whole. Every organism, every phenomenon is something beautiful and not fully understood. Life is the main miracle.

Programming & Math

Mathematics is the science of everything, describing objects and events, allowing us to contemplate the immensity with the help of numbers and functions. But I'm a creator by nature, so what, if not programming, could captivate me even more?

Movies

Watching movies for me is rather not a vacation, but a kind of activity. I try to allocate time only to those films that make you look at things differently, rethink what seemed obvious

Herbert Wells Writer

Herbert George Wells (21 September 1866 – 13 August 1946) was an English writer. Prolific in many genres, he wrote dozens of novels, short stories, and works of social commentary, history, satire, biography and autobiography. His work also included two books on recreational war games. Wells is now best remembered for his science fiction novels and is often called the "father of science fiction", along with Jules Verne and the publisher Hugo Gernsback.
